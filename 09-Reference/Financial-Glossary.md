# Financial Glossary

Quick reference for financial terms used across Warren's agents and reports.

## Tax Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| AGI | Adjusted Gross Income — total income minus above-the-line deductions |
| AMT | Alternative Minimum Tax — parallel tax system to prevent high earners from using deductions to pay too little |
| Estimated Tax | Quarterly tax payments for income not subject to withholding |
| FICA | Federal Insurance Contributions Act — Social Security + Medicare taxes |
| Filing Status | Tax classification (Single, MFJ, MFS, HoH) that determines brackets and deductions |
| Itemized Deductions | Specific deductions listed on Schedule A (mortgage interest, SALT, charitable, etc.) |
| K-1 | Schedule K-1 — reports income/deductions from partnerships, S-corps, trusts |
| NIIT | Net Investment Income Tax — 3.8% surtax on investment income above thresholds |
| Pass-through Income | Business income that flows through to the owner's personal return |
| QBI | Qualified Business Income — eligible for 20% deduction under Section 199A |
| Safe Harbor | Minimum payment threshold to avoid estimated tax penalties |
| SALT | State and Local Tax — deductible on federal return, capped at $10,000 |
| SE Tax | Self-Employment Tax — Social Security + Medicare for self-employed individuals |
| Standard Deduction | Fixed deduction amount based on filing status (alternative to itemizing) |
| W-2 | Wage and Tax Statement from employer |
| 1099 | Information return reporting non-employment income |
| 1040-ES | Form for making estimated tax payments |

## Financial Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Cash Flow | Movement of money in and out of accounts over time |
| Liquid Assets | Cash and assets easily convertible to cash (checking, savings, money market) |
| Net Worth | Total assets minus total liabilities |
| Savings Rate | Percentage of income saved (not spent) |
| Budget Variance | Difference between budgeted and actual spending |
| Recurring Expense | Regular, predictable expenses (rent, subscriptions, insurance) |
| Discretionary Spending | Non-essential spending (dining out, entertainment, shopping) |

## Account Types

| Type | Description | Tax Treatment |
|------|-------------|---------------|
| 403(b) | University retirement plan (similar to 401k) | Pre-tax contributions, taxed on withdrawal |
| Roth IRA | Individual retirement account | After-tax contributions, tax-free growth and withdrawal |
| Traditional IRA | Individual retirement account | Pre-tax contributions (if eligible), taxed on withdrawal |
| Solo 401(k) | Self-employed retirement plan | Pre-tax or Roth contributions |
| SEP IRA | Simplified Employee Pension | Pre-tax employer contributions only |
| HSA | Health Savings Account | Triple tax advantage: deductible, grows tax-free, tax-free for medical |
| 529 | Education savings plan | After-tax contributions, tax-free for education expenses |
