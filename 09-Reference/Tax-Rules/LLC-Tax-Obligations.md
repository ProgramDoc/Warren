# LLC Tax Obligations — AiTheia LLC

Reference guide for LLC tax obligations. The specific treatment depends on AiTheia's entity classification (to be confirmed during financial intake).

## Entity Classification Options

### Single-Member LLC (Default)
- **Federal**: Disregarded entity. All income/expenses reported on Tom's Schedule C (Form 1040)
- **Self-employment tax**: Yes, on net business income
- **California**: $800 minimum franchise tax + LLC fee based on gross receipts
- **Tax documents**: No separate federal return; income flows to personal return

### LLC Taxed as S-Corporation (S-Corp Election)
- **Federal**: File Form 1120-S (due March 15). Income passes through to Tom via K-1
- **Reasonable salary required**: Tom must pay himself a "reasonable" W-2 salary
- **SE tax savings**: Only W-2 salary is subject to FICA; distributions are not
- **California**: $800 minimum franchise tax + 1.5% tax on net income (S-Corp tax)
- **Tax documents**: Form 1120-S + Schedule K-1 to Tom

### LLC Taxed as Partnership (Multi-Member)
- **Federal**: File Form 1065 (due March 15). Income passes through via K-1
- **Self-employment tax**: Yes, on Tom's distributive share of net income
- **California**: $800 franchise tax + LLC fee based on gross receipts
- **Tax documents**: Form 1065 + Schedule K-1 to each partner

## Common LLC Deductions

| Deduction | Description | Limit |
|-----------|-------------|-------|
| Ordinary & necessary business expenses | All costs of running AiTheia | Must be reasonable |
| Home office | If Tom uses dedicated space for AiTheia | Simplified: $5/sqft (max $1,500) |
| Health insurance | If LLC pays Tom's health insurance (SE deduction) | 100% of premiums |
| Retirement contributions | SEP IRA, Solo 401(k), or SIMPLE IRA through AiTheia | Varies by plan type |
| Vehicle expenses | Business use of personal vehicle | Standard mileage or actual |
| Professional development | Courses, conferences related to AiTheia's business | Must be directly related |

## Key Planning Considerations

### S-Corp Election Benefits
If AiTheia's net income is significant (generally > $50K-$60K after salary), an S-Corp election may save on self-employment tax. The tradeoff:
- **Saves**: FICA on distributions above reasonable salary
- **Costs**: Additional filing (1120-S), payroll administration, reasonable compensation analysis
- **CPA should evaluate**: Whether the tax savings justify the administrative burden

### Retirement Plan Options Through AiTheia
| Plan | Max Contribution (2026) | Notes |
|------|------------------------|-------|
| SEP IRA | 25% of net SE income (max ~$69,000) | Simple to set up, employer-only |
| Solo 401(k) | $23,500 employee + 25% employer (max ~$69,000 total) | More complex, allows loans |
| SIMPLE IRA | $16,500 employee + 3% employer match | Lower limits |

### Quarterly Estimated Payments
If AiTheia is a disregarded entity or partnership:
- Tom makes estimated payments personally (1040-ES)
- Based on total income including AiTheia pass-through

If AiTheia is an S-Corp:
- AiTheia withholds payroll taxes on Tom's W-2 salary
- Tom may still need estimated payments for distributions and other income

## California-Specific LLC Requirements

- **$800 annual franchise tax**: Due April 15 every year, regardless of income
- **LLC fee**: Additional fee based on total income from California sources (see California-Tax-Rates.md)
- **Statement of Information**: File with CA Secretary of State every 2 years
- **Business license**: May be required by city (Los Angeles business tax)
