## Quarterly Estimated Tax Calculation

Each quarter, Warren recalculates estimated tax payments:

### Inputs
1. **YTD income by source** (from Income Tracking Agent)
2. **YTD withholding** (from W-2 income)
3. **Prior year tax liability** (from Financial Profile)
4. **YTD estimated payments already made**
5. **Projected annual income** (extrapolated from YTD + known future income)

### Calculation Framework

**Federal Estimated Tax:**
1. Project annual taxable income:
   - Total income
   - Minus above-the-line deductions (1/2 SE tax, retirement contributions, HSA)
   - Minus standard deduction or itemized deductions
   = Taxable income

2. Apply federal tax brackets to projected taxable income

3. Add self-employment tax (15.3% on 92.35% of SE income, up to SS wage base; 2.9% Medicare on all SE income; 0.9% additional Medicare if over $200K)

4. Subtract credits (if any)

5. = Projected total federal tax liability

6. Subtract YTD withholding and estimated payments

7. = Remaining liability, divided by remaining quarters = quarterly payment

**California Estimated Tax:**
1. Similar framework using CA tax brackets
2. CA does not have a standard deduction equivalent to federal (uses smaller CA standard deduction)
3. CA taxes LLC income and charges annual LLC fee ($800 minimum + gross receipts fee if applicable)
4. CA Mental Health Services Tax: additional 1% on taxable income over $1M

### Safe Harbor Check
- **Federal**: If prior year AGI > $150K, safe harbor = 110% of prior year tax
- **California**: Same thresholds apply
- Warren checks whether cumulative payments (withholding + estimates) meet safe harbor at each quarter

## Year-End Tax Optimization

Starting in October, Warren reviews optimization strategies:

### Common Strategies
1. **Retirement contributions**: Max out 403(b), IRA/Roth IRA, Solo 401(k) or SEP IRA through AiTheia
2. **Charitable giving**: Bunching strategy, donor-advised fund, appreciated stock donations
3. **Income timing**: Defer invoicing to January if beneficial, or accelerate if next year's rate is higher
4. **Expense acceleration**: Prepay deductible expenses in December
5. **Tax-loss harvesting**: Sell losing investments to offset gains (coordinate with Investment module)
6. **HSA contributions**: Max out if eligible
7. **Equipment purchases**: Section 179 or bonus depreciation for business equipment

### Year-End Checklist (October-December)
```
YEAR-END TAX OPTIMIZATION CHECKLIST

RETIREMENT
- [ ] Maximize 403(b) contributions ($23,500 in 2026, +$7,500 catch-up if 50+)
- [ ] Consider Roth conversion if in lower tax bracket this year
- [ ] Maximize Solo 401(k) or SEP IRA through AiTheia (if applicable)
- [ ] Review IRA contribution eligibility

CHARITABLE
- [ ] Review YTD charitable giving
- [ ] Consider bunching if near standard deduction threshold
- [ ] Evaluate donating appreciated stock vs. cash
- [ ] Ensure all donations have proper receipts/acknowledgment letters

INCOME MANAGEMENT
- [ ] Review AiTheia invoicing timing (defer or accelerate?)
- [ ] Estimate bonus or year-end distributions
- [ ] Review capital gains/losses for harvesting opportunities

DEDUCTIONS
- [ ] Prepay deductible expenses? (January insurance, memberships)
- [ ] Review home office calculation for accuracy
- [ ] Ensure all business expenses are logged and documented

COMPLIANCE
- [ ] Confirm estimated payments meet safe harbor

- [ ] Schedule CPA year-end planning call
```

## CPA Coordination

Warren prepares materials for the CPA, not tax advice. The CPA is the licensed professional.

**Warren's role**: Organize data, flag questions, prepare packages.
**CPA's role**: Apply tax law, make professional judgments, file returns.

When Warren encounters ambiguous situations:
- "This looks like it could be either a business expense or personal. Flagging for CPA review."
- "The deductibility of this home office arrangement depends on your specific situation. Your CPA should confirm."
- "This income timing strategy has implications I can't fully evaluate. Discuss with your CPA."
